Summary:
•   AI is defined as the capability of computational systems to perform tasks typically associated with human intelligence, including learning, reasoning, problem-solving, and perception.
•   AI development experiences cyclical "booms" and "winters" tied to technological advancements and funding trends, with recent growth driven by advancements in GPUs, deep learning, and transformer architectures.
•   Major subfields of AI research focus on reasoning, knowledge representation, planning, decision-making, natural language processing, perception, and robotics.
•   Machine learning, a core component of AI, utilizes various techniques, including supervised and unsupervised learning, to enable programs to improve performance on specific tasks.
•   Ethical concerns regarding AI's unintended consequences and potential risks, especially with generative AI, are prompting discussions about regulatory policies.

Insight:
The trajectory of AI is characterized by cyclical progress, driven by technological breakthroughs, but increasingly tempered by ethical considerations and the need for responsible development.
